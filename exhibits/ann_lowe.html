Ann Lowe

Learn about Ann Lowe and three of her gowns designed for Gasparilla celebration - including 3D scans of the gowns.

Special Exhibition

About the Exhibit

The Henry B. Plant Museum highlights the career of Ann Lowe, the first internationally recognized Black fashion designer, who revolutionized couture with her signature floral embellishments. It explores her profound impact on Tampa’s high society, where she operated a premier dressmaking salon and became the commissioned dressmaker for the prestigious Gasparilla Court in 1924.

The presentation showcases how her craftsmanship transcended social barriers, creating gowns for elite clientele including future First Ladies and Oscar winners. It also features three preserved Gasparilla gowns that illustrate the transition of her work from early 1920s silk designs to the intricate, beaded satin of the late 1950s.
