MR. SKINNER, AT YOUR SERVICE

The Labor of Fine Dining at the Tampa Bay Hotel

Special Exhibition

About the Event

This exhibit, “Mr. Skinner, at Your Service,” explores the labor of fine dining at the Tampa Bay Hotel between 1891 and 1932. It highlights leaders like Oscar E. Skinner, who managed the hotel's complex hierarchy of kitchen and dining room staff, navigating a world where seasonal migration between northern and southern resorts was the norm. The presentation shows how the labor of chefs, bakers, and waiters evolved as the hotel transitioned from an elite winter retreat to a destination for the growing middle class. It also features the contrasting experiences of African American waiters, whose professional expertise in the dining room was often overshadowed by the hotel’s demands that they perform racial stereotypes for guest entertainment.
