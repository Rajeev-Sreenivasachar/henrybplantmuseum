About the Event

Start your weekend at the Henry B. Plant Museum. In addition to the Museum’s permanent and rotating exhibits, enjoy each month’s special program. Events range from short films to performances to curator conversations.

Friday, April 3, 4:30-7:00 pm

Experience history through the words of the Tampa Bay Hotel’s most famous authors as Curator Selena Martinez discusses the legacy of public readings and performs works by icons Stephen Crane and Jose Marti.

Friday, May 22, 4:30-7:00 pm

To honor the founding of the American Red Cross, this special lecture highlights Clara Barton’s role during the War of 1898 and her organization's lasting influence on the City of Tampa as well as the Tampa Bay Hotel.

Event Details

Date: One Friday of Every Month

Time: 4:30 PM – 7:00 PM

Location: Henry B. Plant Museum, 401 W Kennedy Blvd, Tampa, FL 33606

Ticket: This performance is included with museum admission and seating is provided on a first‑come, first‑served basis.

What to Expect

  • Learn more about the museum's history and its past
  • Experience different types of programs each time
  • Explore the exhibits of the museum

Visitor Info

Admission: The event is free with the museum admission.

Accessibility: Wheelchair accessible; early arrival recommended for best seating.
