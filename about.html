The Museum’s Legacy

The Henry B. Plant Museum is a historic landmark that showcases Tampa’s past through exhibits, events, and artifacts.

The Birth Of The Tampa Bay Hotel

Built in 1891 by Henry B. Plant, the Tampa Bay Hotel featured electric lighting, elevators, telephones, and running water.

Iconic Architecture

The hotel’s Moorish Revival architecture and elegant furnishings made it one of Florida’s most famous hotels.

Preserving Tampa’s Past

Today, the museum preserves artifacts and history, allowing visitors to experience the true essence of the Gilded Age.

Our Mission

To preserve and celebrate the history of the Tampa Bay Hotel and Henry B. Plant while inspiring appreciation for Florida’s cultural heritage.

Why Visit Us?

Interactive Exhibits

Hands-on exhibits that bring the Gilded Age to life for visitors of all ages, connecting the past to the present.

Educational Programs

Programs designed for students, families, and history enthusiasts exploring Florida’s rapid growth and development.

Historic Preservation

Original furnishings, breathtaking artwork, and priceless artifacts meticulously preserved from the 1891 hotel.
